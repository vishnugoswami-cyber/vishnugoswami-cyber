## What is the Internet?
- The Internet is a giant network that connects millions of 
  computers and devices worldwide
- It allows devices to communicate and share information

---

## Types of Networks

### 🏠 Private Network (LAN)
- Used within homes, schools, or offices
- Not accessible from outside
- Example: Your home WiFi network
- IP range: 192.168.x.x

### 🌍 Public Network (WAN)
- Accessible by anyone on the internet
- Example: The internet itself
- Your ISP (Internet Service Provider) connects you to it

---

## How to Identify a Device on a Network

### 🔢 IP Address
- IP = Internet Protocol
- Acts like a **home address** for your device on a network
- Example: 192.168.1.1
- Two types:
  - **IPv4** → 192.168.1.1 (most common)
  - **IPv6** → longer, newer format

### 🔑 MAC Address
- MAC = Media Access Control
- A **unique permanent ID** burned into your network card
- Never changes (unlike IP)
- Example: a4:c3:f0:85:ac:2d
- Used to identify devices within a local network

---

## IP vs MAC - Key Difference
| | IP Address | MAC Address |
|---|---|---|
| Changes? | Yes | No |
| Used for | Internet routing | Local identification |
| Assigned by | Router/ISP | Manufacturer |

---

## 🧠 Key Takeaways
- Every device needs an IP to communicate online
- MAC address is the physical identity of a device
- Private networks are local, public networks are global
